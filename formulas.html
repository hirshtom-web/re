Investment & Homeowner Metrics Explained

Cash Flow

Cash flow represents the money left over each month after all property expenses are paid. Positive cash flow indicates a profitable property, while negative cash flow means you’re paying more than you earn.

Formula:

Cash Flow = Rental Income – (Mortgage + Taxes + Insurance + Maintenance + Other Expenses)

Key Words:

Rental Income Mortgage Taxes Insurance Maintenance

Return on Investment (ROI)

ROI measures the efficiency of your investment. It shows how much profit you earn relative to the money you’ve invested in the property.

Formula:

ROI (%) = (Annual Cash Flow ÷ Total Investment) × 100

Key Words:

Annual Cash Flow Total Investment Down Payment
tips_and_updates Measure investment efficiency

Cap Rate

Cap rate measures annual return relative to property value, ignoring financing.

Formula:

Cap Rate (%) = Net Operating Income ÷ Property Value × 100

Key Words:

Net Operating Income Property Value

Cash-on-Cash Return

Cash-on-Cash return measures ROI only on the actual cash invested, including financing.

Formula:

Cash-on-Cash (%) = Annual Pre-Tax Cash Flow ÷ Cash Invested × 100

Key Words:

Annual Pre-Tax Cash Flow Cash Invested

Gross Rent Multiplier (GRM)

Quick estimate of property value relative to rent.

Formula:

GRM = Property Price ÷ Annual Gross Rental Income

Key Words:

Property Price Annual Rent

Occupancy Rate

Shows what percentage of units or rental time is occupied.

Formula:

Occupancy Rate (%) = (Occupied Units ÷ Total Units) × 100

Key Words:

Occupied Units Total Units

Mortgage Payment

Monthly payment including principal, interest, taxes, and insurance.

Formula:

Mortgage Payment = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance

Principal Interest Taxes Insurance

Equity

Portion of home owned outright.

Formula:

Equity = Home Value – Mortgage Balance

Home Value Mortgage Balance

Price-to-Rent Ratio

Compare buying versus renting a home.

Formula:

Price-to-Rent Ratio = Home Price ÷ Annual Rent

Key Words:

Home Price Annual Rent

Appreciation

Measures property value growth over time.

Formula:

Appreciation (%) = (Current Value – Purchase Price) ÷ Purchase Price × 100

Key Words:

Current Value Purchase Price

Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)

Shows ability to handle mortgage payments.

Formula:

DTI (%) = Total Monthly Debt Payments ÷ Gross Monthly Income × 100

Monthly Debt Gross Income
